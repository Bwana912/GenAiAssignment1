# Expanding Access to AI

My name is Bill Bwana. I was born in Kenya and now live in the United States, where I am pursuing a Master's in Information Systems and AI for Business. I'm especially interested in machine learning, open-source development, and practical ways to make technology useful for people and organizations that are usually left out of major tech shifts.

The problem area I care most about is widening access to AI. As someone who has seen different levels of access across countries and communities, I care about whether new tools reach only large, well-funded organizations or also support small nonprofits, schools, and underserved communities. One challenge is that the people building and funding these systems often come from a narrow set of backgrounds, which affects which problems are prioritized and which communities are overlooked. Long term, I want to contribute through open-source work, advanced study in AI/ML, and a nonprofit tech startup focused on broader access.

**Three ways generative AI could help in this area:**

- Code assistants can help small organizations and nonprofit teams build useful tools faster, even when they do not have large engineering staffs or consulting budgets.
- Generative AI can create training materials, translate explanations, and simplify technical content, making it easier for learners from different regions and backgrounds to participate in AI-related work.
- Smaller open-source teams can use generative AI to draft documentation, summarize project discussions, and speed up repetitive coding and communication tasks that would otherwise slow them down.
